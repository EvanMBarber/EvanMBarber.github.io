Welcome to Star Grazer page.

Welcome to the official development site for Star Grazer, a pixel-based space adventure crafted by strongNebulaForge Studios. Here, we share updates, showcase artwork, and connect with players and creators who are helping shape the universe of Star Grazer. From concept art to community ship designs, every pixel brings us one step closer to launch. Explore the sections below to learn more about our vision, our process, and how you can be part of the Star Grazer community.

“Every star you graze leaves a trail of destiny.” — NebulaForge Studios

Who are we?

Star Grazer was created by NebulaForge Studios, a small team of indie developers and digital artists who share a passion for retro-style games and pixel art. Our mission is to bring creativity and community together by providing a space where players and artists can design, showcase, and submit custom pixel ships for use in upcoming space shooter projects. What started as a simple idea between friends has grown into a collaborative hub for artists around the world who love turning tiny pixels into massive imagination.

Our team of talented developers

Markus is the lead programmer at NebulaForge Studios, where he brings the technical heart of Star Grazer to life. With a strong background in gameplay systems and optimization, Markus specializes in turning creative ideas into smooth, responsive code. He’s passionate about blending old-school game mechanics with modern design practices, drawing inspiration from the 16-bit arcade titles he grew up playing. When he’s not coding late into the night, Markus enjoys experimenting with new game engines and sharing development tips with the indie dev community.

Jeff is the sound designer for NebulaForge Studios and the creative force behind every blast, hum, and melody in Star Grazer. With years of experience in digital audio production, he crafts immersive soundscapes that bring each pixelated battle and cosmic journey to life. Inspired by the chiptune tracks and synth-heavy arcade music of the early ’90s, Jeff combines retro tones with modern mixing techniques to give Star Grazer its signature sound. When he’s not composing, he’s usually tinkering with vintage keyboards or sampling everyday noises for his next in-game sound effect.

Emily is the lead artist at NebulaForge Studios and the visionary behind the distinct look of Star Grazer. Her love for pixel art began with sketching spaceships and characters on graph paper as a kid, which evolved into a full-time passion for digital art and game design. Emily’s attention to color, motion, and atmosphere gives Star Grazer its nostalgic yet vibrant identity. She draws inspiration from classic arcade shooters and early console games, blending that retro energy with modern flair. When she’s not designing new assets, Emily can be found illustrating concept art or mentoring up-and-coming pixel artists in the community.

What are we making?

Star Grazer is a 2D open-world, top-down shooter built in GameMaker Studio 2. Players pilot customizable ships through sprawling galaxies, battling enemies, completing missions, and uncovering secrets hidden across the stars. Our goal is to capture the thrill and challenge of classic arcade shooters while blending them with modern open-world exploration.

As developers at NebulaForge Studios, our passion for game creation was born in the arcades of 1994 — the flashing lights, roaring soundtracks, and fast-paced action that defined our childhoods. Star Grazer is our tribute to that golden era of gaming, reimagined for a new generation of players who love both nostalgia and innovation.
